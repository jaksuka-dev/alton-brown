Recipe: Appetizing Pasta 'n Broccoli and Sweet Ham

  • Alton Brown
  • Thursday, Jun 11, 2020

Pasta ‘n Broccoli and Sweet Ham. Creamy Ham & Peas Pasta – Cream Sauce Pasta – Ham Pasta. Chicken, Mushrooms, Broccoli and Carrot Stir Fry – Delicious! Satisfy your comfort food cravings with this cheap and simple creamy pasta dish with chunks of ham and crunchy veg.

Cook the pasta, quickly steam the broccoli and stir-fry the ham and garlic for a few minutes. The simple sauce is made with heavy cream and Parmesan cheese. Or omit the ham and it instantly becomes a delicious vegetarian dish. You can cook Pasta ‘n Broccoli and Sweet Ham using 6 ingredients and 4 steps. Here is how you achieve that.

Ingredients of Pasta ‘n Broccoli and Sweet Ham

  1. Prepare 250 g of Spaghetti Pasta.
  2. Prepare 5 slices of broccoli.
  3. Prepare 400 g of Sweet Ham (can also be filipino longganisa).
  4. It’s 3 tbsp of Apple Cider Vinegar.
  5. You need 1 pinch of Salt.
  6. It’s 2 tbsp of Extra Virgin Olive Oil.

Feel free to use mixed vegetables or peas instead of broccoli. Ham broccoli pasta casserole is wonderful for using up leftover ham after the holidays or anytime you just want a good casserole to serve family and friends. You might like to check out my recipe for sweet cornbread muffins to go with this casserole. Learn how to make Creamy Pasta Shells with Broccoli and Ham.

Pasta ‘n Broccoli and Sweet Ham step by step

  1. Boil water with 1 tsp salt and 2 tbsp oil. Add pasta. Let it boil until the pasta is cooked. Once, pasta is cooked add the broccoli just to dip it in a boiling water for 1min. Then drain the pasta and broccoli from boiling water and put it aside to cool..
  2. In another pan, add 1/8 water to cook the sweet ham ‘til the water dried out from boiling then the sweet ham is ready..
  3. Slice the sweet ham and broccoli. Mix the rest of the ingredients in a bowl..
  4. Serve hot (or cold still tasty)..

Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Return empty pasta pot to medium-low heat. Add broccoli and ham and cook until hot. Except I bought “John Morrell julienne stripped ham”. Within minutes of being done, the sauce quickly dried up.
